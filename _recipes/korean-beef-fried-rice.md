## Ingredients

- 750g Lean Beef Mince
- 80g Low Sodium Soy Sauce
- 25g Honey
- 4-5 Garlic Cloves Minced
- 30g Gochujang (Brand: Huaypo)
- 10ml Sesame Oil
- Sesame Seeds
- Green Onion
- 10ml Olive Oil
- 1 Medium Onion chopped
- 1 Carrot Grated
- 3-4 Whole Eggs (can use egg whites)
- 500g Cooked White Rice
- 50g Light Mayo
- 20g Gochujang
- 1/2 tsp Garlic Powder
- 10g Rice Vinegar

## Instructions

1. When making the sauce, combine low sodium soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, gochujang, and sesame oil. Use half of it for the beef and the other half for the fried rice.
2. When cooking the beef, make sure most of the water has evaporated before adding the sauce. Let it cook with the sauce for a few mins till golden brown and crispy.
3. For the fried rice, heat olive oil in a pan, add chopped medium onion and grated carrot and sauté. Cook the eggs (whole or egg whites) on low heat till scrambled.
4. Add the second half of the sauce to the 500g cooked white rice, make sure it’s well combined and cook for 3-4 mins before adding the beef.
5. Combine light mayo, gochujang, garlic powder, and rice vinegar to make the Spicy Korean Sauce. Serve together topped with green onion and sesame seeds.

## Notes

> To add more volume to the sauce, mix a little water with it. You can use egg
> whites instead of whole eggs to save calories.
