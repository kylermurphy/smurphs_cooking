## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken (about 1.5kg), or 1kg bone-in, skin-on thighs
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1cm coins
- 3 celery stalks, sliced 1cm thick
- 1 large onion, quartered (no need to peel)
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
- 200g egg noodles or small pasta (orzo, ditali)
- Small bunch of flat-leaf parsley, stems and leaves separated
- Fine sea salt
- 2 litres cold water

## Instructions

1. Place the chicken in a large pot with the onion, garlic, parsley stems, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Pour over the cold water — starting cold draws more flavour from the bones than starting hot. Season with 1½ tsp salt.

2. Bring to a bare simmer over medium heat. Do not let it boil hard or the broth will turn cloudy. Skim off any grey foam that rises to the surface in the first 10 minutes.

3. Partially cover and simmer gently for 1 hour, until the chicken is completely tender and beginning to fall from the bone.

4. Carefully lift the chicken out and set aside to cool for 10–15 minutes. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot, discarding the aromatics.

5. Taste the broth and season generously with salt — it should taste deeply savoury. This is the most important step.

6. Bring the strained broth back to a simmer. Add the carrots and celery and cook for 8 minutes, until just tender.

7. While the vegetables cook, shred the chicken meat from the bones with two forks, discarding the skin and bones.

8. Add the noodles to the pot and cook according to package instructions. Return the shredded chicken to the pot for the last 2 minutes to warm through.

9. Ladle into deep bowls. Finish with torn parsley leaves and a crack of black pepper. Serve with crusty bread.

## Notes

> **Make ahead:** The broth (steps 1–5) can be made 3 days ahead and refrigerated. Skim the solidified fat from the surface before reheating — or leave it for richer soup.
>
> **Noodles:** Cook the noodles separately if you plan to have leftovers. They absorb liquid and turn mushy when stored in the broth.
>
> **Freezing:** The broth + chicken (no noodles) freezes well for up to 3 months.
