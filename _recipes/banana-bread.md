## Ingredients

- 3 very ripe bananas (about 300g peeled)
- 75g unsalted butter, melted and cooled
- 150g golden caster sugar
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
- Pinch of fine sea salt
- 190g plain flour

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to `175°C / 350°F`. Grease a 9×5 inch loaf tin and line with baking parchment.

2. Mash the bananas in a large bowl until completely smooth — a few small lumps are fine, but the smoother the mash, the more evenly it will distribute through the batter.

3. Stir in the melted butter until combined. The mixture will look a little curdled; that's normal.

4. Add the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Mix until everything is incorporated.

5. Sprinkle over the bicarbonate of soda and salt and fold in gently.

6. Sift in the flour and fold until just combined. **Do not overmix** — stop as soon as you can no longer see dry flour, or the bread will be tough.

7. Pour the batter into the prepared tin and smooth the top with a spatula.

8. Bake for 55–65 minutes, until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. If the top browns too quickly, tent loosely with foil after 40 minutes.

9. Cool in the tin for 15 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack. Slice once fully cool for cleaner edges.

## Notes

> **Storage:** Keeps well wrapped at room temperature for 3 days, or freeze individual slices for up to 3 months.
>
> **Variations:** Fold in 75g chocolate chips, or 60g toasted walnuts at step 6.
>
> **Banana tip:** If your bananas aren't ripe enough, place unpeeled bananas on a baking tray at `150°C` for 15 minutes until black. Cool completely before using.
